KANDAWGYI

Kandawgyi Lake is one of Yangon’s most peaceful natural spaces, surrounded by wooden walkways, gardens and a calm lakeside atmosphere. Many people visit just to relax, walk, take photos or enjoy the scenery. However, the wooden paths and lakeside areas are getting older and need better care. Many experts say the park also has huge potential to become a learning area about nature and water ecosystems.

  • Mingalar Taung Nyunt Township
  • Near Yangon Zoo
  • 2–3 minute walk from Kantharyar centre
  • Open: 5 AM – 9 PM
  • Wooden lakeside bridge
  • Karaweik Palace view
  • Calm lake scenery
  • Shady walking paths
  • Great for: peaceful walks, photography, quiet picnics
  • Repair and protect wooden walkways using sustainable materials.
  • Install small nature-education signs about lake wildlife and plants.
  • Create shaded resting areas to encourage longer visits.
  • Design an “eco-walk trail” with information about water conservation.

INYA

Inya Lake is one of Yangon's most iconic outdoor spaces, famous for its long walking paths, cool breezes, and beautiful sunsets. People come here to jog, cycle, relax with friends, or simply enjoy the peaceful environment. However, some areas lack shade, certain paths are worn out, and waste bins are not always placed in effective locations. With thoughtful upgrades, Inya can become a top recreation and fitness destination.

  • Along Pyay Road / Kabar Aye Road
  • 20-minute walk from Yankin center
  • Open: 4:30 AM – 10 PM
  • Long lakeside path
  • Windy sunset views
  • Natural relaxing atmosphere
  • Great for: jogging, cycling, sunset watching, relaxing with friends
  • Plant more shade trees and add comfortable seating along the lakeside.
  • Create a dedicated bicycle lane for safe cycling.
  • Build a “sunset-view platform” to attract tourism and photography.
  • Improve placement of recycling bins and water refill stations.

MAHAR BANDULA

Located in the heart of downtown Yangon, Mahar Bandula Park is surrounded by important landmarks such as City Hall, the Supreme Court, and Sule Pagoda. Because of its central location, many people use it for short breaks, photos, or gatherings. But the open grassy space is often damaged by heavy foot traffic and the central area becomes very hot during the day. With better design and greenery, this park can become a historical and community-focused attraction.

  • Downtown Yangon (next to Sule Pagoda)
  • 10-minute walk from Sule Bus Stop
  • Open: 6 AM – 8 PM
  • Independence Monument
  • Big open lawn
  • Surrounded by historic buildings
  • Great for: relaxing breaks, group photos, short hangouts
  • Add shade trees and seating areas to reduce heat.
  • Create a “History Walkway” with signs describing nearby heritage buildings.
  • Improve night lighting for safety and evening activities.
  • Protect grass by designing proper footpaths and designated sitting areas.

PEOPLE'S PARK

People’s Park is one of the largest and most diverse parks in Yangon, filled with flower gardens, open lawns, walkways and a clear view of Shwedagon Pagoda. The park is excellent for families, students, and large events. Although it is already well-developed, some corners of the park lack maintenance and could be improved to increase educational and tourism value.

  • Dagon Township
  • Near Shwedagon Pagoda
  • 10-minute walk from Dagon centre 2
  • Massive open park area
  • Clear view of Shwedagon
  • Colourful seasonal flower gardens
  • Great for: picnics, family outings, taking photos, group activities
  • Create themed gardens (butterfly garden, fragrance garden, bird garden).
  • Add updated playground equipment made from safe materials.
  • Install heritage tree labels to educate visitors about old species.
  • Use compost from fallen leaves to support flower gardens.

THUWANA

Thuwana Park is a quiet, nature-filled space known for its jogging tracks and wide green fields. It is popular for morning exercise, group workouts and peaceful afternoon walks. However, the park lacks shade, comfortable resting areas, and environmental awareness activities. With a few improvements, Thuwana can become a strong community health and eco-friendly recreation area.

  • Thingangyun Township
  • Near Thuwunna Stadium
  • 30-minute walk from Thuwana Youth training centre
  • Shaded jogging trail
  • Quiet, calming environment
  • Open green field
  • Great for: jogging, morning exercise, quiet relaxing, long walks
  • Plant more large shade trees along jogging paths and open fields.
  • Add fitness stations for stretching, cardio and strength training.
  • Improve mini lakes using native plants to protect water quality.
  • Place bottle-collection and recycling points near exercise areas.
