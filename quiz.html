Park Adventure Quiz

Explore the park and click a hotspot to answer a question — test your eco-knowledge!

Click the hotspot to take the quiz.

Tree Zone

Which action helps trees stay healthy in parks?

Try again — harming bark damages trees. Correct — watering and protecting roots helps growth. Try again — removing leaves stresses the tree.

Hint: Keep soil loose and water young trees in dry months.

Recycle Zone

Where should plastic bottles be thrown?

Correct — plastics should go to recycling. Try again — leaving trash harms the park. Try again — never throw trash in water.

Hint: Look for the green recycle icon when you visit.

Pond Zone — Q3

Why should ponds be kept clean?

Correct — clean water supports aquatic life. Try again — not all parks allow fishing. Try again — this isn't why ponds are preserved.

Hint: Don’t feed wildlife — leftover food harms water quality.

Bench Area — Q4

What should you do when you see a broken bench?

Correct — reporting helps staff fix hazards. Try again — unsafe to use broken furniture. Try again — never damage park property.

HInt: Many parks have contact info posted at entrances.

Path Zone

Which is the best travel option to reduce pollution to the park?

Try again — cars increase pollution. Correct — walking or biking reduces pollution. Try again — older engines pollute more.

Hint: Encourage friends to cycle or walk to the park!

Garden Zone

Why are native plants important in parks?

Try again — native plants usually need less water. Correct — they support local biodiversity. Try again — this is not true for many native species.

Hint: Native plants are often easier to care for.

Compost Zone

What should be done with park food waste?

Try again — composting is better. Correct — compost returns nutrients to the soil. Try again — feeding wildlife can harm them.

Hint: Compost bins turn waste into healthy soil for gardens.

Birds

Why should we not disturb bird nests?

Correct — nests are important for baby birds. Try again — aesthetic isn't a reason to disturb them. Try again — disturbing wildlife for photos is harmful.

Hint: Keep distance and enjoy wildlife quietly.
