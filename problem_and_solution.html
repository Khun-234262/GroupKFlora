Park Problems & Solutions

Kandawgyi Park

Problems

  • Damaged wooden walkways create safety risks.
  • Lack of clear navigation signs for tourists.
  • Poor waste-bin placement causes scattered litter.

Solutions

  • Repair and reinforce walkways using durable materials.
  • Add multilingual direction boards (Eng + Burmese).
  • Install eco-friendly recycling & trash bins every 50 meters.

Inya Lake Park

Problems

  • Overcrowding during evenings and weekends.
  • Few safe walk/bike lanes near busy roads.
  • Not enough seating areas.

Solutions

  • Design time-based crowd zones (walking / jogging / relaxing).
  • Add protective fences & painted bike lanes along roads.
  • Build shaded seating areas using sustainable materials.

Mahabandoola Park

Problems

  • Very limited green shade due to few trees.
  • Too noisy because of downtown traffic.
  • Benches and pathways are old and damaged.

Solutions

  • Plant large local trees for natural cooling.
  • Add noise-reducing plant walls and hedges.
  • Renovate walkways with smooth, durable stone.

People’s Square Park

Problems

  • Huge area but very few clear direction signs.
  • Some areas feel unsafe at night due to poor lighting.
  • Drinking water stations are limited.

Solutions

  • Add simple illustrated maps at every entrance.
  • Install solar-powered lighting along pathways.
  • Create refillable water stations to reduce plastic use.

Thuwunna Park

Problems

  • Playgrounds are old and unsafe.
  • Pond water is polluted and stagnant.
  • Few shaded areas for families.

Solutions

  • Renovate playgrounds with safe rubber flooring.
  • Add water filtration + plant-based purification systems.
  • Build bamboo shade shelters and tree planting zones.
